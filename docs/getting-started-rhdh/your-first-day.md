## Logging In

Your first step is accessing Red Hat Developer Hub. The login process depends on how your organization has configured authentication.

### Accessing Developer Hub

1. **Navigate to your Developer Hub URL** - Your administrator will provide you with the URL (e.g., `https://developer-hub.yourcompany.com`)
2. **You'll see the login page** - The exact login options depend on your organization's configuration

### Authentication Methods

Your organization may use one of several authentication methods:

**Enterprise Authentication**

* Single Sign-On (SSO) with your corporate credentials
* Federated authentication (e.g., Keycloak, Ping Federate)
* OAuth providers (e.g. GitHub)

**Guest Access**

* Some instances allow guest access for read-only exploration
* Guest users typically have limited permissions

!!! tip "Need Help Logging In?"
    If you're having trouble logging in, contact your administrator or check your organization's internal documentation for authentication setup instructions.

### After Logging In

Once you successfully log in, you'll be taken to the **Homepage**‚Äîyour central dashboard in Developer Hub.

## Exploring the Homepage

The homepage is your starting point in Red Hat Developer Hub. After logging in, you'll see a personalized dashboard that provides an overview of your organization's software ecosystem and quick access to commonly used features.

### Welcome Greeting

At the top of the homepage, you'll see a personalized greeting:

* **"Welcome back!"** followed by a time-based greeting (e.g., "Good afternoon, [Your Name]!")
* This greeting helps you feel at home and confirms you're logged in correctly

### Getting Started Cards

The homepage features three prominent action cards to help you get started:

**Get Started**

* Includes a **"Read documentation"** button that opens the [official documentation](https://docs.redhat.com/en/documentation/red_hat_developer_hub)
* Perfect for understanding what Developer Hub can do for you

**Explore**

* Includes a **"Go to Catalog ‚Üí"** button that takes you directly to the [Software Catalog](/catalog)
* Great for discovering what's available in your organization

**Learn**

* Includes a **"Go to Learning Paths ‚Üí"** button that opens [learning path](/learning-paths) resources
* Ideal for building your Developer Hub skills

!!! tip "Start with the Cards"
    These three cards provide the fastest path to getting productive. Click through each one to familiarize yourself with the platform's key features.

### Explore Your Software Catalog

Below the getting started cards, you'll see a section showcasing your organization's software catalog:

**Catalog Overview Card**

* A large card showing an illustration and text: "Browse the Systems, Components, Resources, and APIs that are available in your organization."
* A link at the bottom shows the total number of catalog entities (e.g., "View all 22 catalog entities")
* Click this link to see the full Software Catalog

**Featured Components**

* Two or more component cards displayed side-by-side
* Each card shows:
  * Component name and description
  * Tags (e.g., "System", "Component", technology tags)
  * Quick preview of what the component does
* Click any component card to view its full details

!!! success "Discover Your Ecosystem üåê"
    The homepage gives you a snapshot of your organization's software. Use it to discover components, understand relationships, and find resources you need.

### Explore Templates

Further down the homepage, you'll find the **"Explore Templates"** section:

* Shows available [software templates](/create) you can use to create new projects
* Templates help you bootstrap new services following your organization's best practices
* Click through to browse and use templates

!!! info "Customizable Dashboard"
    Your organization may customize the homepage with additional widgets or sections specific to your needs. What you see depends on your role, permissions, and organizational configuration.

### Navigating from the Homepage

From the homepage, you can:

* **Click on component cards** to view their full details
* **Use the action buttons** on the getting started cards to jump to key features
* **Access the Software Catalog** via the "View all" link or "[Catalog](/catalog)" in the left navigation
* **Browse templates** through the Explore Templates section
* **Start using templates** through the Self-Service button (`+`) in the Global Header
* **Access your starred items** via the star icon (‚≠ê) in the Global Header

!!! tip "Bookmark the Homepage"
    Consider bookmarking your Developer Hub homepage in your browser for quick access throughout your day. It's your central hub for everything in Developer Hub.

## Using Quickstarts

Quickstarts are simple tasks that encourage you to explore Developer Hub's features. They appear in a dock on the right side of the homepage, providing quick introductions to key capabilities and direct links to try them out.

### What Are Quickstarts?

Quickstarts are brief introductions to Developer Hub features. Each quickstart:

* **Explains what a feature does** - Gives you context about why it's useful
* **Encourages you to visit it** - Provides a direct link or button to explore the feature
* **Helps you discover capabilities** - Introduces features you might not know about

Think of them as friendly suggestions that guide you to explore different parts of Developer Hub.

### Accessing Quickstarts

Quickstarts are located in the **right sidebar dock** of the homepage:

1. **Look for the dock on the right side** of the homepage
2. **See the heading**: "Let's get you started with Developer Hub"
3. **Read the subtitle**: "We'll guide you through a few quick steps"
4. **Browse the list** of available quickstart items below

### Available Quickstarts

The quickstarts dock can be customized by your administrator and often contains list of features available to you. Examples may include:

**Foundation Quickstarts:**

* **What is Developer Hub?** - Understand the platform's purpose and capabilities
* **Navigate Your Way Around** - Learn about the homepage, topbar, and navigation features
* **Browse The Software Catalog** - Discover components, services, and APIs
* **Browse The 'Golden Path' Templates** - Learn about software templates

**Content Quickstarts:**

* **Read Some TechDocs** - Access and read technical documentation
* **Explore APIs** - Discover and test APIs in your organization

**Productivity Quickstarts:**

* **Ask Lightspeed To Help You** - Get AI-powered assistance (if available)
* **Add Software To The Catalog** - Register components to the catalog
* **Personalize Your Experience** - Customize your Developer Hub settings
* **Learn New Skills** - Access learning paths and training
* **Star Your Favorite Items** - Create quick access to frequently used resources

**Advanced Quickstarts (for administrators):**

* **Manage Developer Roles and Permissions** - Configure access control
* **View Your Platform Adoption Metrics** - Monitor platform usage
* **Manage and Install Plugins** - Extend Developer Hub capabilities

### Using Quickstarts

To use a quickstart:

1. **Find the quickstart** you want in the right sidebar dock
2. **Click on the quickstart item** to expand it
3. **Read the brief explanation** - Learn what the feature does and why it's useful
4. **Click the action button** (e.g., "Start Exploring") - This takes you directly to the feature
5. **Explore the feature** - Try it out and see how it works

!!! tip "Start with Foundation Quickstarts"
    If you're completely new to Developer Hub, start with "What is Developer Hub?" and "Navigate Your Way Around". These will help you understand the basics before exploring more advanced features.

!!! success "Learn by Exploring üöÄ"
    Quickstarts are designed to get you hands-on with features quickly. Don't worry about mastering everything‚Äîjust click through and explore. You can always come back to learn more details later.

### Quickstart Status

The quickstarts dock shows your progress:

* **"Not started"** - Quickstarts you haven't explored yet
* **Completed** - Quickstarts you've visited

You can hide the quickstarts dock using the "Hide" link at the bottom if you want more screen space, and bring it back later when needed by clicking the question mark (?) in the GlobalHeader.

!!! tip "Work Through Them at Your Pace"
    You don't need to complete all quickstarts at once. Work through them as you need to learn about different features, or use them as a checklist to ensure you've explored all of Developer Hub's capabilities.

## Managing Starred Items

Starred items are your personal favorites‚Äîcomponents, APIs, documentation, or other resources you want quick access to. Think of them as bookmarks within Developer Hub.

### What Can You Star?

You can star various types of resources:

* **Components** - Services, applications, and libraries you work with frequently
* **APIs** - APIs you use regularly
* **Documentation** - TechDocs you reference often
* **Templates** - Templates you use frequently

### How to Star an Item

**From a Component or Resource Page:**

1. Navigate (or search) the catalog and open a component, API, or documentation page
2. Look for the **star icon** (‚≠ê) near the title or in the header
3. Click the star to add it to your starred items

### Accessing Your Starred Items

Once you've starred items, you can access them quickly:

**From the Global Header:**

1. Look for the **star icon** (‚≠ê) in the Global Header
2. Click it to see all your starred items
3. Click any item to navigate directly to it

!!! success "Build Your Workspace ‚≠ê"
    Star frequently used components, APIs, and documentation. This creates your personal workspace within Developer Hub, making it easy to access what you need most.

### Unstarring Items

To remove an item from your starred list:

1. Navigate to the item
2. Click the star icon again (it will be filled/highlighted if starred)
3. The item is removed from your starred items

!!! tip "Organize Your Workflow"
    Use starred items to create quick access to:
    
    * Components you're actively working on
    * APIs you're integrating with
    * Documentation you reference regularly
    * Templates you use frequently

## Your Profile and Settings

Developer Hub provides two ways to manage your account: **Settings** for personalization and **User Profile** for viewing your information and relationships.

### Settings Page

Access settings by clicking your **avatar or name** in the Global Header and selecting **"Settings"**.

The Settings page includes:

**Appearance**

* **Theme** - Switch between Light, Dark, or Auto (follows system preferences)
* **Language** - Change the interface language
* **Pin Sidebar** - Toggle to keep the sidebar always visible

!!! tip "Customize Your Experience"
    Choose a theme that's comfortable for your work environment. Many developers prefer dark mode for extended coding sessions.

**Developer Hub Identity**

* View your user entity and ownership entities (clickable links to your profile)

**RHDH Version Info**

* View TechDocs builder and authentication provider information
* View the Developer Hub version (handy for reporting issues etc.)

### User Profile Page

Access your profile by clicking your **avatar or name** in the Global Header and selecting **"My Profile"**.

Your profile shows:

**About**

* Links to view the source of your profile and any TechDocs associated with it
* Your description, owner, and tags etc.

**Ownership**

* Toggle between **Direct Relations** and **Aggregated Relations** to see what you own
* Visual grid showing counts of components, APIs, documentation, and other entities you're connected to

!!! success "Understand Your Relationships üîó"
    The Ownership section helps you see everything you're connected to in Developer Hub. Use the toggle to switch between direct and aggregated views.
